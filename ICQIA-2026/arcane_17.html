How · the engines & the bridge

Three solvers, one goal: reach the lowest energy.

Optimization is the immediate commercial catalyst. Three families of quantum solver attack it — each evaluating a vast landscape of interconnected variables at once, tunneling toward the global minimum that classical heuristics miss.

QAOA

gate-based
|γ,β⟩ = ∏k e−iβkHMe−iγkHC|+⟩n

Alternating cost & mixer layers; a classical optimizer tunes the angles.

strengths
  • 2p tunable angles · depth p
  • p=1 MaxCut ratio ≥ 0.6924
  • multi-angle ≈ 3× shallower circuits
userouting · scheduling · max-cut

VQE

hybrid
E(θ) = ⟨ψ(θ)|H|ψ(θ)⟩ ≥ E0

A classical loop drives a quantum ansatz to the lowest eigenstate of H.

strengths
  • variational principle bounds E0
  • noise-resilient, shallow ansätze
  • measures expectation values directly
usechemistry · materials · catalysis

Annealing

analog
H(t) = (1−s)HM + s·HC

Slowly morph from mixer to problem Hamiltonian; the system relaxes to its ground state.

strengths
  • tunnels through energy barriers
  • physical ground-state relaxation
  • D-Wave Advantage2 · 4,400+ qubits
uselogistics · QUBO / Ising at scale

How · the biggest race — error correction

Many noisy qubits → one that doesn't fail.

Decades apart

AI was named in 1956; quantum computing began with Feynman in 1981. For ~40 years they advanced on separate tracks — one in software, one in physics.

Shared mathematics

Both run on linear algebra — state vectors, matrices, gradient optimization. Quantum just operates in an exponentially larger Hilbert space.

AI rescued the hardware

From ~2018, deep-learning decoders & AI compilers — AlphaQubit (2024), YAQQ — made noisy NISQ qubits usable. Quantum's progress now rides on ML.

Now quantum reshapes AI

Since 2024–25, native spectral/kernel ops & quantum agents (QKSA) open learning regimes classical AI can't reach — the two tracks have finally merged.

How · anatomy of a quantum computer

The "chandelier" is mostly a fridge.

  • Plates cool the chip in stages: 4 K → 15 mK — colder than deep space
  • Gold-plated coax lines carry the microwave pulses that gate qubits
  • The processor is the fingernail-sized chip at the very bottom
  • Everything above preserves coherence — superconducting T1 ≈ 100 µs
  • Shielding blocks thermal & magnetic noise that would collapse the state
gate fidelity → 99.99% (IonQ) · 99.921% 2-qubit (Quantinuum Helios)
interesting facts
  • 15 mK is ~250× colder than deep space (2.7 K)
  • Cooling uses a ³He/⁴He dilution cycle — no moving parts at the cold stage
  • Qubits are driven by microwave pulses near 5 GHz
  • The whole fridge can weigh hundreds of kg for a chip you can't see

the scaling catch

Each logical qubit needs ~1,000 physical — so wiring & refrigeration, not the chip, become the bottleneck. A key reason photonic & networked approaches are gaining ground.
