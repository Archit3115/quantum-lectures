## 2. The global inflection — 2025 was the year quantum "got real"

Three peer-reviewed firsts in a single year moved quantum advantage from promise to measured fact:

- **Google Willow (Nature, Dec 2024):** 105 superconducting qubits; a random-circuit benchmark in <5 minutes vs ~10²⁵ years classically. Crucially the **first below-threshold error correction** — logical error *shrinks* as the code grows (suppression factor Λ ≈ 2.14 per code step).
- **D-Wave Advantage2 (Science, Mar 2025):** magnetic-materials simulation in minutes on a 4,400+ qubit annealer — a real scientific target, though parts were later matched on GPUs (contested).
- **Google "Quantum Echoes" (Nature, Oct 2025):** first *verifiable* quantum advantage (~13,000×) via out-of-time-order correlators — checkable on a second machine, a path toward molecular-structure readouts.

**Market signal (McKinsey Quantum Technology Monitor 2026):**
- Up to **$2.7 trillion** of *economic value* worldwide by 2035 (chemicals, life sciences, finance, mobility capture most).
- The **internal market** (hardware + software + services) reaches **$60–100B by 2035**, of which quantum computing is **$43–71B**.
- Quantum-computing companies generated **>$1B revenue in 2025**, projected to **$4.4B by 2028**.
- Start-up investment hit **$12.6B in 2025 — 6.3× the 2024 figure — with ~90% flowing to quantum computing.**
- **BCG** (complementary view): **$450–850B** economic value by 2040, sustaining a **$90–170B** provider market.
- **Talent:** industry projects **~250,000 new jobs by 2030, ~840,000 by 2035**; in 2025 only ~5,000 qualified workers were available against ~10,000 needed — a shortage the US has called a "national security vulnerability." **This gap is precisely where an FDP matters.**

*Speaker line:* "The value is not in selling processors — it is in the outcomes. Hardware enables; the software, algorithms and AI layers capture the value. That layer is a keyboard-and-brains problem — which is exactly India's strength."

## 3. India's National Quantum Mission (NQM) — the spine of the story

- **Approved 19 April 2023; ₹6,003.65 crore; runs 2023-24 → 2030-31 (8 years).** Anchored in DST; builds on the earlier QuEST programme and the NM-ICPS quantum hub (Rs 170 cr) — "one dovetails into the other."
- **Staged compute targets:** 20–50 physical qubits in 3 years → 50–100 in 5 years → **50–1,000 in 8 years**, across superconducting and photonic platforms.
- **Communication:** satellite-based secure QKD over 2,000 km; inter-city QKD over 2,000 km on existing fibre with trusted nodes; multi-node quantum networks with quantum memories.
- **Sensing & metrology:** magnetometers at 1 fT/√Hz; atomic clocks at 10⁻¹⁹ fractional instability; gravity sensors.
- **Materials & devices:** superconductors, topological materials, semiconductor structures for device fabrication.

### The four Thematic Hubs (T-Hubs) — hub-and-spoke, each a Section-8 company
| Hub | Lead institution | Mandate |
|---|---|---|
| **Quantum Computing** | **IISc Bengaluru** (Foundation for QC Innovation) | Indigenous quantum processors; the staged 20→1000 qubit targets |
| **Quantum Communication** | **IIT Madras + C-DOT, New Delhi** | QKD, secure networks, satellite links |
| **Quantum Sensing & Metrology** | **IIT Bombay** | Magnetometers, atomic clocks, navigation |
| **Quantum Materials & Devices** | **IIT Delhi** | Qubit materials, cryo-compatible devices |

- **IIT Kanpur** is the management coordinating centre.
- Scale: **152 researchers, 43 institutions, 14 technical groups, across 17 states + 2 UTs** — a genuinely national footprint (relevant to *every* college in the room).
- Sanctioned amounts include Rs 589.40 cr (Sensing) and Rs 312.79 cr (Materials) to the respective hubs.

### Indigenous ecosystem — "shipping, not slideware"
- **QpiAI (Bengaluru, f. 2019):** India's first full-stack quantum computer — **25-qubit "Indus"** (Apr 2025); **64-qubit "Kaveri"** (Nov 2025); custom decoder cut error-correction decoding latency **60 µs → 1.5 µs** (Mar 2026, claim not yet fully documented publicly); **$65.6M raised, ~100 staff, roadmap 1,000 qubits by 2030.**
- **IISER Pune (Prof. Umakant Rapol):** 25 calcium ions trapped; **20-qubit ion-trap machine due end-2026** — a flagship NQM deliverable.
- **Amaravati Quantum Valley (Andhra Pradesh, launched 7 Feb 2026):** partners **IBM, TCS, L&T**; anchored by an **IBM Quantum System Two** (India's largest to date); first indigenous machine targeted Dec 2026. *(Caveat: cite "IBM Quantum System Two, India's largest" — public qubit-count figures vary, ~133–156.)*
- **Startup layer:** 8 startups selected (~₹30 cr each) — **Dimira** (indigenous cryogenic cables), **QNu Labs** (QKD), **QUTE Electronics** (precision power supplies), and others building real components.
- **Milestones (2026):** ~**1,000 km quantum communication** demonstrated (Apr 2026); a **national post-quantum cryptography (PQC) roadmap** (Feb 2026) to protect Aadhaar-scale identity and UPI-scale payments *before* the threat matures.
- Minister Jitendra Singh (Feb 2026): India has reportedly met **roughly half of NQM's targets within three years**.

### The honest number (use it — faculty respect candor)
India's public quantum funding (~**$0.73B / ₹6,003 cr over 8 years**) sits against **China ~$15B** (reputed), **UK £2.5B/10yr**, **US $2.5B+**, **Germany ~€3B**, **France PROQCIMA** (targeting 128 logical qubits by 2030). *India is funded to compete selectively in chosen niches — not to outspend anyone.* Its edge is **algorithms, software, and a vast talent pool** — the value-capturing layer.

## 4. Viksit Bharat 2047 — where quantum fits

**Viksit Bharat @2047** = a fully developed India by the centenary of independence: economic prosperity, social advancement, environmental sustainability, effective governance. The through-line for this audience:

- India has already built **planet-scale digital public infrastructure** — Aadhaar (biometric identity), UPI (real-time payments), Digital India. That is the *demonstrated* ability to take a frontier technology to population scale.
- The next leap is from **"world's back office" → "world's innovation engine"**: frugal *jugaad* efficiency is necessary but no longer sufficient; deep R&D, frontier science, and **sovereign tech ecosystems** are the 2047 demand (BW / Prof. Arindam Ghosh, IISc).
- Quantum sits inside a coordinated mission stack: **NQM + IndiaAI Mission + National Supercomputing Mission + Semicon India + ANRF/RDI Scheme** (₹1 lakh crore RDI fund) — technology as a strategic asset for growth, security, governance and global influence.
- **IBM framing:** "Becoming a leader in quantum computing will prove central to [Viksit Bharat]... India can leverage its distinguished research institutions and software/algorithm expertise to lead in the era of quantum *algorithm and application* discovery."
- **The gap to close (be honest):** R&D spend must rise toward **2–3% of GDP by 2047**; reverse brain drain; create high-tech jobs. Every faculty member in the room is a lever on the talent side of that equation.

*Closing speaker line:* "You are not spectators to this. The single largest bottleneck globally is people who understand this stack. Every course you seed, every student you point at QML, is India's comparative advantage compounding. Viksit Bharat 2047 will be built by the cohort sitting in your classrooms right now."

## 5. Industry inevitability — why this is not optional

- **Cryptography / security:** "Harvest-now-decrypt-later" makes PQC migration urgent *today*; India's Feb-2026 PQC roadmap protects UPI/Aadhaar rails.
- **Drug discovery & materials:** quantum simulation of molecules/catalysts — energy & materials industries are "uniquely positioned" (McKinsey); directly relevant to pharma-strong India.
- **Logistics & mobility:** optimization (routing, scheduling, supply chains) — the near-term commercial catalyst.
- **Finance:** portfolio optimization, risk, Monte Carlo — a major value pool.
- **AI × Quantum (this FDP's theme):** quantum kernels/feature maps for structured data; AI decoders (AlphaQubit) rescuing noisy NISQ hardware. The two fields have merged.

**Bottom line for slide use:** classical HPC is hitting physical limits (end of Dennard/Moore scaling); AI's compute hunger is outpacing silicon; quantum opens a *new axis* (hyper-dimensional, not merely smaller). For India, the winning move is the **algorithm-and-application layer** — which is what QML, clustering, and quantum-enhanced vision (the two sessions) actually are.
